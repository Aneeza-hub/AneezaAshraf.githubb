# Project Reflection

---

**Project Title:** Impacts of Social Media on Mental Health

---

## My Role in the Project

In this project, I contributed in a dual capacity — as both a developer and a researcher. My responsibilities included developing a thorough understanding of the problem domain, analyzing the documented effects of social media on mental health, and supporting the formulation of solutions grounded in research findings. I also played an active role in organizing and structuring information to ensure the project remained focused and aligned with its core objectives.

---

## Project Overview

The primary purpose of this project was to investigate how social media usage affects mental health, with particular attention to younger users. The study examined both the positive and negative dimensions of social media engagement — including its role in facilitating communication and connection, alongside its well-documented associations with addiction, anxiety, chronic stress, and diminished self-esteem.

---

## Challenges Faced

The project presented a number of meaningful challenges, including:

- Developing a nuanced understanding of the psychological impact of social media
- Sourcing reliable, relevant, and academically credible data
- Managing time effectively while balancing concurrent academic responsibilities
- Coordinating task distribution and maintaining consistent communication within the team

---

## How Challenges Were Resolved

These challenges were addressed through a combination of structured research, open discussion, and collaborative teamwork. Referencing peer-reviewed academic articles and real-world studies provided the depth of understanding required to approach the topic with credibility. Systematic planning and a clear division of responsibilities allowed the team to manage time efficiently and maintain steady progress throughout the project.

---

## What I Learned

This project deepened my understanding of technology's real-world influence on human behavior and psychological well-being. It reinforced the importance of designing digital systems with ethical responsibility and user welfare at the forefront. Beyond the subject matter itself, the experience meaningfully strengthened my teamwork, communication, and problem-solving capabilities in a collaborative project environment.

---

## Conclusion

This project was a substantive and enriching learning experience that broadened my perspective on both the technical and social dimensions of computing. It provided a valuable opportunity to connect theoretical knowledge with real-world application — and reaffirmed the responsibility that comes with building technology that directly affects people's lives.
