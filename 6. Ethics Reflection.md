# Ethics Reflection

---

## Ethical Issue Chosen

A significant ethical concern relevant to modern software development is the impact of social media platforms on users' mental health. Many platforms are deliberately engineered to maximize user engagement — a design philosophy that, while commercially driven, can contribute to compulsive usage patterns and, over time, negatively affect the psychological well-being of users.

---

## Problem Explanation

At the core of this issue lies the use of algorithmic systems designed to keep users engaged for as long as possible. While effective from a business perspective, these mechanisms have been linked to heightened levels of anxiety, chronic stress, diminished self-esteem, and reduced productivity — particularly among younger users. The prevalence of curated, idealized content also encourages unhealthy social comparison, further compounding the psychological toll on vulnerable individuals.

---

## Relevant Professional Code

This issue directly intersects with the **ACM Code of Ethics**, which outlines the responsibility of computing professionals to:

- Avoid causing harm to users and the broader public
- Design systems with genuine consideration for user well-being
- Uphold transparency and promote the responsible use of technology

---

## My Decision & Justification

As a future software developer, I firmly believe that user well-being must take precedence over engagement-driven design. Prioritizing metrics such as time-on-platform at the expense of mental health is ethically indefensible. Responsible developers should proactively incorporate features such as usage tracking, screen time limits, content advisories, and algorithmic transparency — not as optional additions, but as fundamental design considerations.

---

## Conclusion

Ethical responsibility is not peripheral to software development — it is central to it. Applications that influence human behavior carry an inherent obligation to protect, not compromise, the well-being of their users. As computing professionals, we must ensure that the systems we build serve people with integrity, care, and a genuine commitment to doing no harm.
